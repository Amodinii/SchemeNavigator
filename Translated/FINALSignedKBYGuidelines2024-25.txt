### **English Translation**

**Krishi Bhagya Scheme Guidelines 2024-25**

No: AGRI-DTE(CROP)/KBMS/2024-25
Date: 17.05.2024

**1. Preamble:**

Since rain-fed agriculture is predominant in Karnataka state, it is essential to undertake agricultural activities by effectively utilizing the rainfall in the state's rain-fed areas. With the great objective of increasing crop productivity, preventing crop failure, stabilizing the livelihood of farmers in the state's rain-fed agriculture, and bringing sustainability to rain-fed agriculture through the adoption of the latest technologies, the Government Order No: KrUI 08 KrUYoKa 2014, Bengaluru, dated 22-07-2014 was issued. As per the said order, the Krishi Bhagya scheme was implemented and has been helpful in increasing (doubling) farmers' income. The scheme was continued from the year 2014-15 to 2020-21 and thereafter. It has been restructured from the year 2023-24.

In Karnataka state, 64% of the cultivated area is rain-fed. Of this, 55% of food grains and 75% of oilseeds are contributed by these rain-fed areas. Since farmers in irrigated areas have an assured water supply, they are compelled to grow crops under irrigation and have thus purchased all suitable equipment, and possess seniority, established practices, and all kinds of knowledge and skills. However, the income of dry-land farmers is dependent on rain, and being unable to solve these problems and lacking other alternative income sources, they experience poverty. To address the problems and challenges of farmers, the Agriculture Department has given more emphasis to drilling borewells deeper than 1000 feet and to the Dr. 2.0 versions. These borewells as sources of irrigation have failed, putting farmers in distress. Therefore, to prevent the hardship of farmers, the Krishi Bhagya scheme is being revised, restructured, and implemented from this year.

In the 2024-25 budget speech, in paragraph 31, the Hon'ble Chief Minister has announced, "The Krishi Bhagya scheme, which was very popular during our government's previous term, will be continued. In the year 2023-24, a grant of Rs. 200 crores was provided, and this scheme will be continued in the current year as well." Accordingly, in the 2024-25 budget, for the implementation of the scheme, a grant of Rs. 100.00 crores has been allocated under the Head of Account: 2401-00-102-0-27. Regarding the implementation guidelines for the Krishi Bhagya Scheme, Government Order No: AGRI/156/AML/2023, Bengaluru.

To be implemented as per order dated 13.12.2023 and amended order dated 14.03.2024.

**Project Proposal:** As per the amendment under the National Disaster Mitigation Fund (NDMF) and in accordance with the 2014 [Act/Guidelines], this will be implemented in 24 districts (106 Taluks) across 5 Disaster Management Zones of the state, covering this district and neighboring districts. In the context of the said region being backward in forestry, agriculture, and animal husbandry, there is a need to provide alternative livelihoods under the Employment Guarantee Scheme (with collaboration between the Central and State governments) as detailed in Annexure-1A to 1E.

**In this district and neighboring districts, programs will be implemented by utilizing the following fund allocations as per the guidelines: The grant has been approved from the National Disaster Mitigation Fund (NDMF), State Disaster Mitigation Fund (SDMF), Comprehensive Environment Plan for Mining Impact Zone (CEPMIZ) for mining-affected, backward areas, and other schemes.**

**1. Project Goal:**
Similar to Ballari, as per the year 2014, to improve the quality of life of the people in 106 taluks of 24 districts across 5 Disaster Management Zones of the state, and in mining-affected areas, by improving the environment, agricultural development, animal husbandry, and other activities.

**2. Project Objectives:**
1. To bring all districts of the state under comprehensive development.
2. To encourage the partnership of all government departments and public sector enterprises.
3. To strengthen the capacity for planning and implementation, supervision, and evaluation at the district level.

**Details of Works/Activities that can be undertaken under the project in this district and neighboring districts:**
1. Construction of Farm Ponds
2. Water Storage Tank (Farm Pond)
3. Cradle Model Water Storage
4. GI Wire Fencing around the Farm Pond
5. Diesel/Solar/Electric Pump Set to lift water from the pond
6. Micro-irrigation (Drip/Sprinkler) to use the water for agriculture
